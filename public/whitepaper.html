Executive Summary

Most commercial energy conversations focus on the wrong number. The kilowatt-hours (kWh) you consume are only part of your bill. The larger, less visible charge — demand charges — is set by a single 15-minute peak, billed at full rate for the entire month. For hotels, data centers, hospitals, and EV charging operators, this tax on peak moments can represent 30 to 50 cents of every dollar spent on electricity.

Battery energy storage (BESS) targets this charge directly. This whitepaper presents live TrueQuote™ analysis — calculated fresh against NREL ATB 2024 benchmark pricing and real utility rates — for four commercial segments where BESS delivers the clearest, most bankable return.

Hospital & Health System

Representative site · Chicago, IL (60601) · 2,200 kW peak demand · $110,000/mo electric bill

Hospitals are among the most energy-intensive buildings per square foot on earth — MRI machines, surgical suites, HVAC for sterile environments, and 24/7 life-safety systems all running simultaneously. Peak demand is relentless and uncontrollable. ComEd and similar utilities charge hospitals $15–$25/kW/month for demand. For a regional medical center drawing 2,200 kW at peak, that's $33,000–$55,000 in demand charges alone — every single month. BESS also delivers what hospitals need most: zero-gap backup power that's faster, cleaner, and cheaper to maintain than diesel generators.
