# SLIDE 6: HOW IT WORKS (TECHNICAL DEPTH)

## Copy

**Headline:**
TrueQuote™ Multi-Dimensional Optimization

**The Process:**

**INPUTS** → **OPTIMIZATION ENGINE** → **OUTPUTS**

**What Goes In:**

- Utility bills (12 months of usage data)
- Location data (zip code, climate zone, solar resource)
- Building specifications (square footage, load profile, operating hours)
- Energy goals (cost reduction, backup power, sustainability, EV charging)
- Equipment preferences (brands, chemistries, configurations)

**How It Works:**

1. **Load Profile Modeling:** Predicts hourly energy consumption patterns
2. **Tariff Analysis:** Maps utility rate structures (demand charges, TOU rates, peak periods)
3. **Equipment Constraints:** Models real equipment (power ratings, energy capacity, degradation)
4. **Weather Simulation:** Integrates NREL solar production data for location
5. **ITC/IRA Calculations:** Applies federal tax credits (30-70% depending on qualifications)
6. **Multi-Solver Optimization:** Tests thousands of configurations across all asset combinations
7. **IEEE Compliance Check:** Ensures system meets grid interconnection standards
8. **Financial Modeling:** Calculates NPV, IRR, payback over 25-year project life

**What Comes Out:**

- **System Design:** Optimal combination of solar, battery, generator, EV chargers
- **Equipment Specifications:** Exact kW/kWh ratings, inverter sizing, interconnection requirements
- **25-Year Financial Projections:** NPV, IRR, simple payback, discounted payback
- **Savings Forecast:** Annual savings breakdown (demand charges, energy, peak shaving, grid services)
- **Implementation Roadmap:** Phasing, permitting, utility interconnection timeline

**Key Differentiator:**
Not a simulation tool (like HOMER or Aurora)—a **decision engine with mathematical proof**. Every number is auditable, every recommendation is backed by IEEE/NREL standards.

- **EV-only:** Creates massive demand spikes without storage

1. **Demand Charge Arbitrage** (Battery)
   - Commercial demand charges: $10-$25/kW-month
   - 1 MW battery can reduce peak by 30-40% = $180K-$250K/year

   - Natural gas backup costs 80% less than diesel
   - Hybrid systems: battery for short outages, generator for extended

   - Shift EV charging to off-peak hours
