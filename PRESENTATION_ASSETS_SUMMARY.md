## 📊 HOW TO USE IP IN PRESENTATION

### ✅ DO SAY:

- "We have three compounding moats: technical complexity, data flywheel, and industry trust"
- "Patent-pending multi-system optimization algorithm"
- "TrueQuote™ is a registered trademark"
- "Our load profile database is trade secret IP - 18 months and $2M to replicate"
- "10,000+ quotes create a data moat that compounds monthly"

### ❌ DON'T SAY:

- "We have $30-40M in IP" (self-assessed, VCs won't believe it)
- "Our IP is worth more than our ARR" (red flag for early-stage)
- "We're just building IP to sell it" (wrong motivation)

### 💡 WHEN ASKED ABOUT IP:

**Q: "What's your IP strategy?"**

A: "We have three layers: (1) Patent-pending optimization algorithm - filed provisional, converting to utility patent in Q2. (2) Trade secrets - 18 industry load profiles and 10,000+ utility tariff database, protected via NDAs. (3) Data moat - 10,000 quotes create a dataset competitors can't replicate. The combination is what makes us defensible."

**Q: "What if someone copies your algorithm?"**

A: "Three reasons that's hard: (1) The algorithm is one piece - they'd also need the load profile database (trade secret, 18 months to build), the tariff database (6,000+ rates), and the vendor network (12+ months). (2) We have 10,000 quotes of training data - network effect compounds monthly. (3) We're building trust in a regulated industry - that takes years, not months."

**Q: "Why not patent everything?"**

A: "We patent what we want to block (optimization algorithm). We keep trade secrets what gives us advantage (load profiles, tariff engine). Patents expire in 20 years and reveal your methods. Trade secrets last forever and stay proprietary. It's a strategic mix."
